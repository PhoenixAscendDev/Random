# Purple Hue — Game Concept Document

## Why This Game Exists

*Purple Hue* is a two-player card game about finding someone, understanding them, and discovering what you become together.

The name comes from its ending: one player is blue, the other is red. When they finally meet, their journeys combine into a shade of purple. That hue reflects how well they understood each other, how they changed, and what they chose to bring.

Everything in the game—movement, cards, communication, identity, and building—exists to serve that moment.

---

## What Players Do

Each player builds and explores their own world using cards. They move through that world, gather materials, learn skills, meet characters, and create things.

At the same time, they try to understand the other player without ever speaking to them.

The goal is simple: reach the same hidden meeting place.

The challenge is deeper: arrive with something meaningful for the other person.

---

## The Card Game Foundation

*Purple Hue* is driven entirely by cards.

Players use **Location cards** to build the world. As cards are placed, the board expands into a network of places.

They encounter **Character cards**, which provide side quests. Completing those quests grants **Skill cards**, allowing new ways to move and interact.

Players gather **Materials** from Locations. Materials are used to complete **Build cards**, which create **Structures** that persist in the world and open new paths.

Players also carry and place **Trinkets**, which act as traces of presence.

All movement, interaction, and progress happens through drawing, placing, and interacting with these cards.

---

## The World and Landmarks

Each player builds a separate world, but both worlds are made from the same set of Locations.

Some Locations are **Landmarks**. Landmarks exist in both players’ worlds and act as shared anchors.

When a player interacts with a Landmark, that interaction becomes attached to the Landmark itself. The other player encounters it when they reach that same Landmark in their own world.

This creates a connection between two separate spaces.

---

## Movement, Building, and Progress

As players explore, they:

* Discover new Locations
* Move between Locations
* Gather Materials
* Complete Side Quests
* Gain Skills
* Build Structures

Materials represent effort. Skills represent understanding. Builds bring those together to create something that changes the world.

Through these systems, the world opens over time and new possibilities emerge.

---

## Communication Through the World

Players communicate indirectly.

When a player leaves something at a Landmark, the other player experiences that as a **Disturbance**.

A Disturbance indicates that something has changed at that place. It invites investigation.

When a player investigates, they learn something about what happened and remove the Disturbance.

Through these interactions, players express who they are, what they value, and what they are working toward.

---

## Identity and Change

Each player begins by selecting a Character and defining three Traits that describe who they are.

Each player is also given three Needed Traits. These represent what they are seeking in the other person.

As the game progresses, players may change their Traits through meaningful interactions.

Identity is not fixed. It develops as the player interprets the other and decides who to become.

---

## Needs and Gifts

At the start of the game, each player chooses a Gift they want to receive. This Gift is tied to their Needed Traits and represents something that can be built in the world.

Throughout the game, each player works toward creating Gifts of their own.

A Gift uses the same system as any Build. It requires Materials and Skills and progresses over time.

Some Gifts may be completed. Others may remain unfinished. Each one reflects effort, intention, and interpretation.

Players decide what to pursue and what to leave unfinished.

---

## The Hidden Meeting

At the beginning of the game, a set of possible meeting Locations is prepared. One is selected and kept hidden. This becomes the true meeting place.

Players gain access to this information during the game. When a player learns the meeting location, that knowledge remains private.

From that moment forward, the player carries a new responsibility:

They must guide the other player to that location.

This guidance happens through the existing communication systems of the game.

Players use:

* Actions at Landmarks
* Trinkets
* Builds
* Requests

These become signals that point toward the meeting place.

The other player interprets these signals and decides how to move through their world.

The meeting does not happen automatically. It requires both players to:

* Discover the location
* Understand the signals
* Choose to move toward it

The game ends when both players arrive at the correct meeting place.

---

## The Final Moment — The Hue

When players meet, the full journey is revealed.

Each player reveals:

* Who they were at the beginning (their starting Traits)
* Who they became (their final Traits)
* What they needed (their Needed Traits and chosen Gift)
* What they built (the Gifts they worked toward and their progress)

These elements are placed side by side.

This moment shows more than what was created—it shows how each player changed along the way. It reflects the decisions they made about who to become and how they responded to what they believed the other person needed.

The relationship between Traits and Gifts reveals how each player understood the other:

* Alignment between Needed Traits and final Traits shows how players shaped themselves
* Alignment between Needs and Gifts shows what they chose to create

Together, these form the complete picture of the journey.

One player is blue. The other is red. Their journeys combine into a shade of purple.

That hue reflects:

* Who each player became
* What each player brought
* How closely they understood each other

The hue represents the outcome of the game.

---

## Closing

*Purple Hue* is a card game where players build separate worlds, interpret each other through indirect signals, and work toward bringing something meaningful into a final meeting.

It is about finding someone—and discovering what you become along the way.
